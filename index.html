Questionnaire
Three questions that diagnose the brand pattern
1Brand personality
2Audience feeling
3Twenty year truth
Question 1 of 3
If your brand was a person at a party, which one would it be?
Unmovable
Stays exactly the same regardless of who they're talking to. Consistent, itself, always.
The quiet transformer
Quietly changes how you see something by the end of the conversation.
The one who says it
Says the thing nobody else in the room is willing to say.
Comfortable in chaos
Genuinely at ease when everyone else is looking for the exit.
Question 2 of 3
What do you want someone to feel the moment after they've used your product or engaged with your brand?
Settled
Like something has been resolved. A door has closed in the best possible way.
Unsettled in a good way
Like something has opened up. A door has opened they didn't know was there.
Capable
Like they can now do something they couldn't before. Limitation removed.
Seen
Like the brand understands something about them that others don't.
Question 3 of 3
What would still be true about your brand in twenty years, even if everything else changed?
The quality and consistency
We never compromise on the standard. That never changes.
The belief we're built on
The product will change but the conviction won't.
The refusal
There will always be something we won't do that others will.
The tension we hold
We won't resolve something the market wants us to resolve.

Phase trigger met
Scale — Phase 2 unlocks
Same constraint. Same truth. More of everything that's working. Nothing that wasn't working gets more budget.
Phase trigger not met
Diagnose — do not scale
Identify which condition failed. Fix the root cause before adding budget.
If signal not met — diagnose first
ROAS failing
Channel or audience targeting problem. Review channel selection before increasing spend.
CAC failing
Offer or landing page problem. Fix the funnel, not the spend.
CTR failing
Creative or truth problem. Go back to the brief. Revisit the visual mechanic before increasing budget.
Sell-through failing
Product or pricing problem. Never increase paid spend to solve a product-market fit problem.

What feeds forward
Performance learnings into next brief
Which formats worked. Which channels over-performed. Which truth type landed hardest. All of this sharpens the next campaign vector brief — not by changing the constraint but by applying it more precisely.
What never changes
The constraint stays fixed
Performance data can refine how the constraint is expressed — never what the constraint is. If the data suggests the brand should express something outside its coordinate, that's a signal the targeting is wrong.
Overlap management
Campaign 1 and 2 run in parallel
Campaign 2 enters production while Campaign 1 is scaling. The Gantt governs the overlap. Both campaigns are governed by the same constraint so there is no coherence risk.
